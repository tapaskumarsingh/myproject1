Understanding HTML Text Formatting Tags

In this example, we will explore the use of the <strong>, <sup>, and <sub> tags in HTML.

Subscript Text

The <sub> tag is used to define subscript text. For example, the chemical formula for water is H2O.

Superscript Text

The <sup> tag is used to define superscript text. For example, the mathematical expression for the square of a number is x2. Another example is the 1st, 2nd, and 3rd ordinal numbers.

Emphasized Text

The <strong> tag is used to emphasize text. This can be useful for indicating important information in a paragraph or drawing attention to a specific part of the text.

Combining Tags

You can also combine these tags for more complex text formatting. For instance, in the chemical formula C6H12O6, the numbers are in subscript, and the formula can be highlighted to indicate its importance.

Similarly, the expression E = mc2 includes a superscript to denote the square of the speed of light, and we can use strong emphasis to emphasize its significance in physics.
